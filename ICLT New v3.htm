ICLT (Information, Communication and Learning Technology) Usage Agreement for Students

Introduction

This agreement covers the use of ICLT facilities provided and maintained by Thomas Rotherham College. Its purpose is to ensure that all ICLT facilities are used in a responsible manner and in a way that covers current legislation, such as the Computer Misuse Act. This document is a guide as to what is deemed 'acceptable use' and the consequences of any misuse. All access to these facilities is monitored and logged so that this agreement can be enforced effectively.

The College has the right to monitor any and all aspects of its telephone and computer systems that are made available to you and to monitor, intercept and/or record any communications made by staff, students and visitors including telephones, e-mail or Internet communications. To ensure compliance with this policy or for any other purpose authorised under the Telecommunications (Lawful Business Practice) (Interception of Communications) Regulations 2000 users are hereby required to expressly consent to the College doing so.

In line with the College's various statutory duties under Safeguarding/Child Protection and under the Prevent agenda concerning the need to prevent people from being drawn into extremism and/or terrorism, the College would need to refer certain suspicious searches or communications to the relevant authorities- police, Local Authority - in circumstances where these gave rise to concerns about the safety and well-being of students and/or others.

The college uses classroom management software (currently AB Tutor) to allow staff to monitor computer usage.

This includes abilities such as:
Remotely viewing/recording your screen
Freezing your access to the computer and taking control of it
Monitoring open applications and web sites
Blocking access to applications, web sites or the internet

In addition the College wishes to make you aware that close circuit television (CCTV) is in operation for the protection of all users of the College and also to protect College assets from theft and vandalism.

Violation of the terms of this agreement is likely to result in disciplinary or other appropriate action being taken by the College.

ICLT facilities include:
Hardware
Software
Systems and services (including the intranet, phones and e-mail)
Peripherals (including printers and scanners)
  1. General Usage
    You must not cause damage of any kind to College ICLT facilities. Use a strong password and keep it private, never let anyone use your account or use theirs. When using College computers, you must not install/download software, attach laptops/mobile phones or eat and drink. Save your work to OneDrive unless told otherwise by staff. All student work saved to the College network is deleted at the end of the year - so you must back this up. The space available for you to save your work on the College network is limited. You must manage this and delete unwanted files. OneDrive is unlimited.
    1. You must not cause any form of damage to ICLT facilities. The term 'damage' includes modifications to hardware or software that, whilst not permanently harming the hardware or software, incur time and/or cost in restoring the system to its original state.
    2. You must ensure that your username and password remain private. You may be held responsible for any inappropriate use or activity carried out using your account details, so if you suspect that someone has discovered your account details you must inform the Computer Services department immediately. Please ensure that:
      • You do not leave your account details in public view
      • Use a strong password that does not contain identifying information such as your student ID number, date of birth or name
    3. You must not attempt by any method to discover another person's account details or attempt to gain access to any of the College's systems using anything other than your assigned username and password.
    4. You must use OneDrive for as your primary storage which can be easily accessed at https://onedrive.live.com or https://thomroth.mydaycloud.com Additional network drives are allocated to some courses, your teacher will inform you if you are on one of these courses and instruct you where to save. Work/data saved on the computer's hard drive (i.e. C:\ drive, This PC, Libraries) may be lost when the computer is re-booted, or cleared as a result of regular maintenance carried out by Computer Services. Home Drives (Z:), departmental drives and course specific drives are backed up by the College and can be restored by Computer Services. Students are encouraged to perform regular backups of anything stored on OneDrive, as the college is unable to restore data stored in this location. Please note – all student drives and collaborative shared drives will be deleted at the end of each academic year. It is the individual student's responsibility to make copies of any files they wish to carry over to the next year. Office365 accounts (location of OneDrive and student email) for students are deleted at the end of December after you leave. The College reserves the right to inspect and access the contents of users' home drives (Z:) at any time.
    5. Disk space is a finite resource. It is your responsibility to manage this resource efficiently. You should delete or archive any old or unwanted files to save disk space. The amount of space you have available may be limited by the allocation of a Disk Quota. Under special circumstances this may be increased at the discretion of the Computer Services department.
    6. You must not install, run or download any software without the prior authorisation of the Computer Services department. This includes freeware, shareware, demo or evaluation software and software that you have personally obtained - even if you have a valid licence. The use on college equipment of any software not covered by an appropriate and valid licence is strictly forbidden. You must not copy any software without appropriate authorisation.
    7. You must not attach any device that is not owned by the College to any computer or to the College network, such as a laptop, without prior authorisation from the Computer Services department.
    8. Food and drink must not be consumed in any classroom/satellite area containing ICLT equipment.
    9. Your computer must not be left unattended. You should log off when you leave your computer.
  2. Mobile Devices
    Your personal mobile device is your responsibility and not the College's and you must use mobile devices and the Wi-Fi system appropriately.
    1. Any mobile device, i.e., laptop/mobile phone, used in College, whether it is your own or College property, is subject to the same usage expectations as that of other ICLT equipment. Similarly when accessing the College Wi-Fi you must adhere to the same internet usage expectations.
    2. College mobile devices available to loan should not be removed from the Learning Resource Centre without consent from the Learning Resource Centre department.
    3. You are responsible for any College mobile device loaned to you and must ensure it is kept secure, is not left unattended and is returned in the same condition in which it was borrowed.
    4. Work must not be saved onto the hard drive of College mobile devices, it should be saved onto OneDrive.
    5. If using your own mobile device on College premises, the College will not accept responsibility for loss of or damage to the mobile device. Liability rests with the owner who should make her/his own arrangements for insurance cover.
    6. The College maintains a high standard of internet security and virus protection, however we will not accept responsibility for any loss of data or damage caused by computer viruses or hacking to your own mobile devices whilst using the College Wi-Fi system on the College premises.
  3. E-mail
    Inappropriate use of e-mail includes unnecessary, unwanted, obscene, abusive messages and messages containing derogatory comments about anyone. You must check the contents of all messages carefully before sending. All e-mails automatically contain the College's standard notice.
    1. E-mail accounts are the property of the College and are provided to assist you in your study/work. You should not assume privacy in any e-mail sent or received. The College reserves the right to inspect the contents of users' mailboxes at any time.
    2. The College reserves the right to retain copies of all e-mails sent and received and to use the contents as evidence in any disciplinary process. The College reserves the right to access and disclose the contents of a user's e-mails in accordance with its legal and audit obligations.
    3. The College may use e-mail to contact you and may pass details of your e-mail address on to third parties at its discretion.
    4. Deliberately creating e-mail congestion by sending trivial or unnecessary messages to multiple recipients will be considered inappropriate use of e-mail facilities.
    5. Repeated sending of unsolicited e-mail to groups or individuals, whether internal or external, after being requested to stop will be considered inappropriate use of e-mail facilities.
    6. You must not send obscene/abusive/disruptive messages via e-mail or another medium.
    7. You should not make derogatory remarks in e-mails about employees, students, competitors or any other persons. Any written derogatory remark may constitute libel.
    8. E-mails should be drafted with care. Due to the informal nature of e-mail, it is easy to forget that it is a permanent form of written communication and that material can be recovered even when it is deleted from your computer.
    9. All e-mails sent outside the College are accompanied by the College's standard notice which currently includes the following statement:
      'This e-mail message (including attachments) is private, is intended for the recipient named in it and may contain information which is confidential and privileged. No-one other than the named recipient my read, copy, rely on, redirect, save or alter the message, any part of it or any attachment to it in any way. Thomas Rotherham College does not accept legal responsibility for the contents of this message. Any views or opinions expressed are solely those of the author and do not represent those of Thomas Rotherham College unless specifically stated. While reasonable effort has been made to ensure that this message is free of viruses, opening and using this message is at the risk of the recipient.'
      The College reserves the right to change this notice at any time.
    10. You should only subscribe to mailing lists which are College related. If subscribing you must only use your own Email address and you must not subscribe to a mailing list which will pass your details to third parties or does not have an effective unsubscribe mechanism. It is your responsibility to unsubscribe from any mailing list as soon as you no longer require it.
  4. Internet
    Internet access is for study purposes and is monitored. Any evidence of inappropriate access will be used, if necessary, for disciplinary action. Inappropriate includes, but is not limited to, pornography, chat, games, gambling, racist/hate speech. When using the Internet, any form of downloading is not permitted including music, video, files and pictures unless they relate to your course.
    1. You must not access any website that is either offensive or illegal. The College has a filtering system to automatically block access to a large number of these sites, however, just because access to a site is allowed via this system it does not mean that a site can be classified as appropriate. The College reserves the right to monitor individual's Internet activity and repeated attempts to access inappropriate sites or attempting to circumvent the College's filtering system will lead to disciplinary action being taken.
    2. The College provides Internet access for study purposes, not recreation. You must not access from the College network, sites deemed as inappropriate by the College. These include, but are not limited to: pornography, chat, games, gambling, racist or hate speech, terrorism and extremism.
    3. You must not download any program without the prior authorisation of the Computer Services department. In addition, the downloading of any files that are not for the purposes of your courses is strictly forbidden. This includes mp3s, video files and pictures.
    4. You must ensure that you adhere to the standard of behaviour as set out in the College's Online Safety Policy, which you are advised to read.
  5. Social Media
    You must take responsibility for your online comments and not post anything that can harm the College and/or its employees/students/visitors
    1. You must ensure you adhere to Appendix 2: Acceptable Online Behaviour of the College's Online Safety Policy. You must ensure you refer to online safety guidelines.
    2. You must not post / re-post any obscene, abusive or disruptive messages online concerning the College or any members of the College community.
    3. You must not make or distribute any derogatory remarks online about employees, students, competitors or any other persons. Any written derogatory remark may constitute libel and may result in disciplinary/legal action.
    4. You are legally liable for anything you post online, therefore careful consideration should be given before uploading information and/or images and consideration should be given to protecting your digital footprint by making use of available privacy settings on sites.
  6. Distance Learning
    You must not record distance learning seminars without express permission. You must conduct yourself as you would in a classroom.
    1. You must not record, take photos or capture in any way digital interactions with your peers or Teachers without the express permission of all involved.
    2. You must treat all learning materials with respect and not alter any live learning documents (e.g. those on OneDrive) that are not for your personal use.
    3. Audio and video seminars are and extension of the classroom, you must conduct yourself as you would in a classroom.
      This includes:
      Being on time for sessions
      Dressing appropriately
      Remaining attentive
      Not using any technology / communications device for purposes unrelated to the session
      Not disrupting the session or the learning of others through poor conduct or misuse of the technology/ functionality of the video conferencing programme/ software
      Interacting patiently and respectfully with my Teachers and Peers
      Providing feedback to Peers and Teachers sensitively
      I will carefully consider the location that I use when video conferencing and ensure that my background is in no way offensive, inflammatory or inappropriate. I will mute myself unless I wish to speak to prevent background noise affecting the session.
      If I become aware that someone is present who I don't believe is a student or member of staff at TRC I will report this immediately to the Teacher leading the session.
      I will end the session as soon as my Teacher indicates to do so and will not stay in the session after the teacher has left. I understand that these rules are designed to help keep me safe and that if they are not followed, members of staff reserve the right to exclude me from the active session and report my conduct through the disciplinary systems.
  7. Copyright
    All online material is copyrighted – you must only use it in your work/study if you have permission. TRC resources such as online learning cannot be shared without express permission.
    1. All text, graphics, pictures, video, sound and any other electronic resources are subject to copyright restrictions and therefore cannot be copied from the Internet unless the copyright holder has granted permission. You are advised to read this information or seek advice if required, as you will be held responsible for any breach of copyright.
    2. The College holds the copyright to all materials produced for College. These materials should not be used for financial gain on sites such as YouTube.
    3. You cannot share resources or videos created by TRC without express permission to do so.
